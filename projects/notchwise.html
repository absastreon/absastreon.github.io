NotchWise

Spaced repetition that disappears into your workflow.

The work

NotchWise was born from a simple observation: Anki's big, attention-grabbing interface breaks flow. You open it to "just do a few cards" and twenty minutes evaporate. The idea behind NotchWise is to put reviews in the last place you'd look — the MacBook notch itself — so sessions become ambient rather than intentional.

A card appears. You hover. You flip. You rate. Back to work. The whole loop takes eight seconds, repeated throughout the day. Thirty cards reviewed by evening, and it never felt like studying.

It's fully offline and local-first — no accounts, no cloud, no telemetry — and runs on every Mac, not just notch MacBooks (a floating notch widget appears on iMacs and older hardware). It imports your existing Anki decks as-is, supports both SM-2 and the modern FSRS scheduler, and integrates cleanly with AnkiConnect so your existing workflow keeps working.

What it does

F.01

Anki-compatible, out of the box

Import .apkg decks, sync via AnkiConnect, use the same SM-2 algorithm or opt into FSRS for better retention modelling.

F.02

Context-aware deck routing

Auto-switches deck based on your frontmost app. Xcode open? Swift cards. Browser on MDN? JavaScript cards. No manual switching.

F.03

Invisible when you need it to be

Hides during screen sharing, Focus modes, and presentations. Flashcards stay private automatically, without a manual toggle.

F.04

On-device AI card generation

Drag text from any app into the notch — Apple's NaturalLanguage framework suggests front/back pairs and cloze deletions. Nothing leaves the machine.

F.05

Pomodoro, built-in

Cards surface during break intervals — so you review when your brain wants to rest, not when you're deep in flow.
